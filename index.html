SvS Code of Conduct

Our Community

It is important to us that Sever vs Server (SvS) fosters a harassment-free environment. Thus, we have designed the following Code of Conduct )CoC) to outline guidelines and expectations that we have for everyone to help us reach this. The CoC also formalises repercussions for breaking these guidelines and provides a point of reference when dealing with issues that may arise in a community of our size.

You can find our full CoC below. If you have any questions or concerns, you can leave them here.

Thank you for helping make this a welcoming, friendly community for everyone.

Scope

This CoC applies to the following people:

  • Staff / organisers
  • Guest speakers / interviewees
  • Competition participants
  • Partners of SvS
  • Members of SvS online spaces
  • Members of participating communities

SvS Spaces

This CoC applies to the following spaces:

  • The SvS Twitch Channel
  • The SvS Discord
  • Public spaces of partnered servers
  • Social Media accounts managed by or that relate to SvS
  • Code repositories, issue trackers and pull requests made against any SvS controlled GitHub organization
  • Interactions with official event accounts on all social media spaces, including comments made on:
    • Event live streams
    • Event video hosting services
    • Official event pages, posts, hashtags etc.
  • Any other online space administered by SvS

This CoC applies to everyone in any of these official SvS online spaces.

Each online space listed above is encouraged to provide the following information to community members:

  • Contact information for any administrators/moderators
  • A welcome message with a link to this CoC and the contact information for making an incident report: https://svs-website.vercel.app/concerns

Additional spaces

Participating Communities

In addition to the SvS-managed spaces listed above, individuals within participating communities are held to the CoC. It is the responsibility of moderators of these spaces to uphold these standards.

Organisers

“Organisers” refer to both Event Staff or Partners.

“Event Staff” are members who are directly associated with SvS and are members of any of the following teams:

  • Event Coordination
  • Creative and Design
  • Social Media
  • Participant Outreach
  • Fundraising and Sponsorship Coordination
  • Live Stream and Tech
  • Moderation

“Partners” are those who are directly involved in the organisation of an event, but are not directly part of the SvS staff team. These can include:

  • Communities/organisations that co-host an event
  • Charities
  • Sponsors

Both kinds of event organisers are expected to:

  • Provide a form of direct contact information to a representative of your organization
  • Uphold and enforce the CoC everywhere - whether directly related to SvS or not
  • Manage events fairly without bias towards or against any participants / participating communities

Behaviours

Members of the SvS community are expected to be open, considerate, and respectful. The following behaviours reinforce these values and contribute to a positive environment:

  • Being open. Members of the community are open to collaboration, compromise, and are committed to transparency. Focusing on what is best for the community. We're respectful of the processes set forth in the community, and we work within them.
  • Acknowledging time and effort. We're respectful of the volunteer efforts that form the foundation of the SvS community. We're thoughtful when addressing the efforts of others, keeping in mind that the labour was often completed simply for the good of the community.
  • Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences. We're receptive to constructive comments and criticism, as the experiences and skill sets of other members contribute to the whole of our efforts. Showing empathy towards other community members. We're attentive in our communications, whether in person or online, and we're tactful when approaching differing views.
  • Being considerate. Members of the community are considerate of their peers -- other SvS participants. Being respectful. We're respectful of others, their positions, their skills, their commitments, and their efforts.
  • Gracefully accepting constructive criticism. When we disagree, we are courteous in raising our issues. Using welcoming and inclusive language. We're accepting of all who wish to take part in our activities, fostering an environment where anyone can participate and everyone can make a difference. Holding members of the community accountable to this CoC.

Standards

Every member of our community has the right to have their identity respected. The SvS community is dedicated to providing a positive and welcoming environment and experience for everyone, regardless of age, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, ethnicity, nationality, religious beliefs, or socioeconomic status.

Inappropriate Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

  • Harassment of any participants in any form (including intimidation or stalking)
  • Logging or taking screenshots of online activity for harassment purposes
  • Publishing other's private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission (“doxing”)
  • Violent threats or language directed against another person
  • Creating additional online accounts in order to harass another person or circumvent a ban
  • Unwelcome sexual advances, languages, or imagery of any kind in online communities
  • Insults, put-downs, or jokes that are based upon stereotypes, are exclusionary, hold others up for ridicule, or are in any way designed to cause offence to a person based upon their identity
  • Disruption of online community discussions, voice calls, or Livestream-based events
  • Unwelcome or inappropriate direct messaging of members without explicit consent
  • Continued one-on-one communication after requests to cease
  • Conduct that violates the Discord, Twitch, or any other platform’s Terms of Service
Disclaimer: We recognise that the use of explicit language/content may be present within a musical/artistic context, as opposed to being used between participating individuals. This extension of artistic license does not override our overall standards and scope and will be carefully reviewed on a case-by-case basis.

Enforcement

If a participant engages in behaviour that violates this CoC, the SvS Moderation team may take action that they deem appropriate, including warning the offender or expulsion from the community and community events. The full list of consequences for inappropriate behavior is listed in the Enforcement Procedures.

Enforcement Procedures

  • We have a zero-tolerance policy regarding discrimination, harassment, or any such targeted behaviours that continue after warning/expressions of discontent.
  • For smaller infractions, up to three warnings will be given, after which proper disciplinary action will be taken.
  • Participants, staff, and partners who are found violating the CoC will be barred from organizing or competing in future SvS events.
  • Event moderators will enforce this code regardless of the event. They will work with the Event Organization team and the parties involved to take a fair course of action.
If you believe that someone is violating the CoC, or have any other concerns, please either use this anonymous form or contact a member of our Event Moderation or Event Staff team immediately.
